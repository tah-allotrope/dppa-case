What are you actually paying per kWh?

Simple buyer view: your DPPA-covered kWh is usually not just the strike price. In the reviewed 2025 examples, it is roughly the strike price plus the regulated DPPA adder, with a small extra effect from market-loss mechanics.

Main risk

Excess CfD

If the contract settles on more generation than you consume, your effective consumed-kWh price rises.

Bottom line

all-in matched kWh ~= strike price + DPPA charge + small basis/loss adjustment
The market-linked charge you pay EVN and the market-price leg inside the CfD mostly cancel each other out. What remains is basically the strike price, plus regulated DPPA system charges, plus a small adjustment from loss and basis effects.

The confusing terms, translated

Term Simple meaning Why you care
Strike price Your negotiated fixed price in the CfD with the generator. This is the commercial anchor price; example reviewed: 2,100 VND/kWh.
FMP Wholesale market reference price used in settlement; one source defines it as SMP + CAN. It determines whether you pay the generator or the generator pays you under the CfD.
CFMP The market-linked EVN PC price for DPPA-covered volume. This is part of what you still pay EVN, even under DPPA.
CDPPAdv / CCL Regulated DPPA system charges and clearing charges. The example materials combine them into a practical 2025 adder of 523.34 VND/kWh.
KPP Loss coefficient multiplier. This is why the exact number is slightly above the shortcut formula.
CBL / retail tariff Normal retail power price for any shortfall between your load and RE generation. Your total savings depend heavily on how much retail tariff you actually avoid.

The buyer payment stack

Total buyer payment = payment to EVN PC + CfD payment to/from generator

Payment to EVN PC

matched volume x CFMP x KPP
+ matched volume x DPPA charge
+ shortfall volume x retail tariff

Payment under the CfD

CfD payment = contracted quantity x (strike price - FMP)
If the strike price is above FMP, you pay the generator. If it is below FMP, the generator pays you.

The practical formula to use

all-in matched kWh ~= (CFMP x KPP + DPPA charge) + (strike - FMP)

If CFMP is close to FMP, this becomes:

all-in matched kWh ~= strike + DPPA charge + small basis/loss adjustment

Fast buyer shortcut using the reviewed 2025 example inputs:

all-in matched kWh ~= strike + 523.34 VND/kWh

The biggest buyer-side commercial risk is not only the strike price. It is whether the settlement quantity can exceed your actual consumption in a trading interval.

What to check before signing

  1. How is the CfD settlement quantity defined: generated volume, allocated volume, consumed volume, or the minimum of those?
  2. Can you be charged on excess generation in intervals when the plant produces more than you consume?
  3. Are CDPPAdv and CCL billed separately or effectively bundled into one adder?
  4. What loss coefficient applies at your voltage level?
  5. What retail tariff do you avoid in each time period when DPPA generation actually matches your load?
